Team 8033 competed at the inaugural Nor Cal District Championship in San Francisco from April 9–12 alongside 59 other Northern California teams. The transition from regional to district competition in California meant Impact Award winners faced additional qualification hurdles — only 2 of 11 Impact teams advanced to Worlds.

Wisp, our robot, performed strongly throughout the season, particularly at San Francisco. The latest design iteration proved inconsistent at the district championship, preventing World qualification. The mentors are proud of how the students grew technically and how much the team's outreach expanded.

## A run of outreach wins

- **Lovat**, the team's scouting app, is now used by over 800 FRC teams worldwide.
- A long-term lease on the **10th Street practice field** was secured through a $245,000 fundraising campaign.
- Student-developed training programs were adopted by **40+ other FRC teams**.
- The team expanded its assistive-device program with OUSD Special Education students.
- Continued support for girls in engineering through badge workshops and STEAM events.
- Community mentoring and tournament volunteering throughout the season.

## On to graduation

We're sending seniors off to **Rice, Georgia Tech, UC Berkeley, and Villanova**. The next generation of Highlander Robotics is already in the room.

If you want to see what the team built in person, come to the **School Maker Faire on May 17, 2026** — Wisp will be there, along with a representative slice of the year's other engineering projects.
